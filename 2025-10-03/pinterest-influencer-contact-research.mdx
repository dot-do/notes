# Pinterest Influencer Contact Information Research Report

**Research Date:** October 3, 2025
**Purpose:** Understand legitimate methods for finding and obtaining Pinterest influencer contact information for business collaboration
**Status:** Research Only - No Implementation

---

## Executive Summary

This report examines legal and ethical methods for obtaining contact information from Pinterest influencers for legitimate business collaboration purposes. Key findings:

- **Pinterest messaging is available** but requires business accounts to opt-in
- **Direct scraping is prohibited** by Pinterest Terms of Service
- **Official API access exists** but is limited to approved partner platforms
- **Third-party influencer platforms** provide the most reliable access to influencer contact data
- **Email finder tools** can locate publicly available contact information
- **Compliance with GDPR/CAN-SPAM** is essential for all outreach

## 1. Where Influencers List Contact Information

### 1.1 Pinterest Profile Sections

**Public Profile Contact Information:**
- Business accounts can add phone numbers and email addresses to their public profiles
- Contact information visibility is optional and user-controlled
- Verified Merchant badges indicate serious business accounts (but verification program is currently paused)
- Merchant details appear below business name for qualified accounts

**Website Links:**
- Claimed websites appear on creator profiles
- Pinterest API can retrieve both claimed and unclaimed website URLs
- Many creators link to professional portfolios or media kits

**Pin Descriptions:**
- Some creators include contact information in Pin descriptions
- Bio sections may contain email addresses or booking links
- Not systematic or reliable for bulk discovery

## 2. Platform-Specific Contact Features

### 2.1 Pinterest Messaging System

**Business Messaging Capabilities:**
- Business accounts can opt-in to receive messages from Pinterest users
- Messaging is **disabled by default** and must be manually enabled
- Adds a "Message" button to the business profile
- Messages appear in Pinterest inbox
- Can block specific users from messaging

**How to Enable Messaging:**
1. Log in to Pinterest business account
2. Navigate to Settings → Social permissions
3. Toggle "Allow others to message my business"
4. Save changes

**Limitations:**
- Only works if the creator has enabled messaging
- No bulk messaging capabilities
- No guarantee of response
- Not visible which accounts have messaging enabled until you try

**Platform Availability:**
- Available on desktop and mobile app
- Similar configuration process across platforms

**Sources:**
- https://help.pinterest.com/en-gb/business/article/messaging-for-your-business
- https://help.pinterest.com/en/article/send-messages

### 2.2 Paid Partnership Program

**How It Works:**
- Partnerships are **established outside Pinterest** first
- Creators tag brand partners in Pins using paid partnership label
- Brands receive notification and must approve the tag
- Brand name appears on Pin after approval
- Only one brand can be tagged per Pin

**Discovery Implications:**
- Pinterest does **not facilitate** initial brand-creator connections
- No built-in marketplace or discovery tool
- Brands must find creators through other means
- Platform only manages disclosure, not matchmaking

**Promotional Features:**
- Brands can promote approved paid partnership Pins as Idea Ads
- Creators cannot promote their own paid partnership content
- Branded Pin management tool in Business Hub for managing requests

**Sources:**
- https://help.pinterest.com/en/business/article/paid-partnerships-for-creators
- https://help.pinterest.com/en/business/article/approve-or-remove-paid-partnership-tags
- https://create.pinterest.com/content-academy/navigating-paid-partnerships-on-pinterest/

### 2.3 Claimed Website Verification

**Website Claiming Process:**
- Creators can claim their websites on Pinterest
- Provides credibility and professional status
- Required for Verified Merchant Program (9+ months domain age)
- Makes websites accessible via Pinterest API

**Benefits for Outreach:**
- Verified websites can be crawled for contact information
- WHOIS database lookups become viable
- Cross-platform profile linking (find them on other social networks)

**Sources:**
- https://help.pinterest.com/en/business/article/verified-merchant-program

## 3. Pinterest API Capabilities

### 3.1 Official Pinterest API (v5)

**Available User Data:**
- Basic account information (username, profile URL, bio)
- Follower counts and following lists
- Board information and Pin data
- Claimed website URLs
- User analytics (engagement rates, impressions, clicks, saves)

**What's NOT Available:**
- Direct email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Private messaging
- Bulk user search/discovery
- Automated follow/unfollow at scale

**Access Requirements:**
- Developer account required
- App registration and approval process
- Rate limits apply
- Terms of Service compliance mandatory

### 5.2 Manual Discovery Methods

**Profile Mining:**
- Check "About" sections on Pinterest profiles
- Look for website links in bio
- Cross-reference with other social platforms (Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn)
- Check Pin descriptions for contact info

**WHOIS Database Lookups:**
- Applicable for claimed websites
- Databases: WHO.IS, GoDaddy, Namecheap
- Provides domain owner contact information
- May be privacy-protected (less useful)

**Cross-Platform Discovery:**
- Find influencer on Instagram → check bio for email
- Check YouTube channel → About section often has business email
- LinkedIn profile → direct messaging or email
- Personal website/blog → dedicated contact page

**Media Kit Discovery:**
- Many professional creators have media kits
- Often linked from social media profiles
- Contains rates, demographics, and contact information
- Search: "[Creator Name] media kit" on Google

### 5.3 Legal Considerations for Email Tools

**What's Legal:**
- Collecting publicly displayed email addresses
- Searching publicly available content
- Using information shared intentionally by creators
- Utilizing publicly accessible databases

**What's Problematic:**
- Automated scraping that violates Terms of Service
- Collecting emails without consent for mass marketing
- Using data obtained through unauthorized means
- Bypassing privacy protections or authentication

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Must provide easy opt-out mechanism
- Clearly identify yourself and business
- Include physical address in emails
- Avoid misleading subject lines
- Honor opt-out requests promptly

## 7. Legal & Compliance Considerations

### 7.1 Pinterest Terms of Service

**Prohibited Activities:**
- **Automated scraping:** "You may not scrape, collect, search, copy or otherwise access data or content from Pinterest in unauthorized ways"
- **Automated access:** Cannot "access data or content by using automated means (without our express prior permission)"
- **Unauthorized data collection:** Any method not explicitly authorized by Pinterest API or Terms of Service

**Commercial Use Requirements:**
- Must create a business account
- Must agree to Business Terms of Service
- Commercial content must follow Community Guidelines, Advertising Guidelines, and Commercial and Branded Content Guidelines
- Accounts for commercial use by anyone under 16 are prohibited

**Enforcement:**
- Pinterest can remove, modify, or limit content distribution
- Accounts can be suspended or terminated
- Legal action possible for Terms violations
- Indemnification clause for unauthorized commercial use

**Sources:**
- https://policy.pinterest.com/en/terms-of-service
- https://business.pinterest.com/business-terms-of-service/

### 7.2 Data Privacy Regulations

#### GDPR (European Union)

**Key Requirements:**
- **Legitimate Interest:** Must have a genuine business reason for contacting specific individuals
- **Data Minimization:** Collect only essential information needed for outreach
- **Transparency:** Clear communication about data processing and opt-out mechanisms
- **Right to Erasure:** Honor deletion requests promptly

**Penalties:**
- Up to €20 million or 4% of annual global turnover (whichever is higher)

**GDPR-Compliant Cold Email:**
- Cold emailing is NOT illegal under GDPR
- Must have legitimate business interest
- Must provide clear opt-out mechanism
- Must be transparent about data usage
- Must honor unsubscribe requests

**Sources:**
- https://secureprivacy.ai/blog/gdpr-compliant-cold-email-guide
- https://mailshake.com/blog/gdpr-compliant-cold-email/
- https://www.lemlist.com/blog/gdpr-and-cold-emailing

#### CAN-SPAM Act (United States)

**Key Requirements:**
- Identify message as advertisement
- Include valid physical postal address
- Provide easy opt-out mechanism
- Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
- Avoid misleading subject lines
- Avoid false header information

**Penalties:**
- Up to $43,280 per violation

**Sources:**
- https://www.growleady.io/blog/cold-emailing-legality-a-must-read-can-spam-gdpr-guide
- https://quickmail.com/cold-email-compliance-and-laws

### 7.3 Anti-Spam Best Practices

**Essential Elements:**
- Clear unsubscribe link in every email
- Transparent sender identification
- Honest subject lines
- Physical mailing address
- Prompt opt-out processing
- Documented compliance procedures

**What to Avoid:**
- Purchasing email lists
- Mass unsolicited emails without legitimate interest
- Misleading claims or false promises
- Hidden unsubscribe mechanisms
- Continuing to email after opt-out request

**Record-Keeping:**
- Document basis for legitimate interest
- Track opt-out requests and compliance
- Maintain records of data collection methods
- Log consent where applicable

**Sources:**
- https://secureprivacy.ai/blog/gdpr-compliant-cold-email-guide
- https://instantly.ai/blog/gdpr-email-marketing/

## 8. Outreach Best Practices

### 8.1 Response Rate Benchmarks

**Industry Averages:**
- Average open rate: **21.5%**
- Average response rate: **1-5%**
- Personalized approaches: **30%+ response rate**
- Well-targeted campaigns: **8-20% response**
- Conversion to partnership: **40-50%** of responders

**Timing Matters:**
- Best days to send: Tuesday through Thursday
- Follow-up timing: 5-7 days after initial email
- Limit to 2 follow-ups maximum

**Sources:**
- https://inbeat.agency/blog/influencer-outreach-email-templates
- https://www.getsaral.com/academy/influencer-outreach-best-practices
- https://sproutsocial.com/insights/influencer-outreach-email-templates/

### 8.2 Email Strategy & Structure

**Optimal Email Length:**
- Target: **75-150 words**
- Emails up to 100 words receive highest response rates
- Shorter and clearer is better

**Subject Line Best Practices:**
- **4-7 words** optimal length
- Personal and clear about opportunity
- 35% of recipients open emails based solely on subject line
- Examples:
  - "Collaboration opportunity for [Creator Name]"
  - "Partnership with [Your Brand]"
  - "[Creator Name] - Brand collaboration"

**Email Structure:**
1. **Personalized greeting** - Use creator's name
2. **Specific reference** - Mention their content you admire
3. **Clear value proposition** - What's in it for them
4. **Brief campaign details** - Keep it high-level
5. **Clear call to action** - Next steps
6. **Professional signature** - Contact info and company details

### 8.3 Personalization Tactics

**Research Before Reaching Out:**
- Review creator's recent Pins and boards
- Understand their audience demographics
- Check past brand partnerships
- Note their content style and values
- Identify alignment with your brand

**Personalization Elements:**
- Reference specific Pins or boards
- Mention why you chose them specifically
- Acknowledge their unique value
- Show familiarity with their content
- Demonstrate brand alignment

**Relationship Building:**
- Seek genuine connection, not transactional relationship
- Show authenticity and trustworthiness
- Provide clear expectations
- Make response easy

**Secondary: Pinterest Messaging**

## 11. Conclusion & Next Steps

### 11.1 Key Takeaways

1. **Pinterest doesn't facilitate discovery** - Partnerships are established outside the platform
2. **API access is limited** - Direct influencer contact data not available via public API
3. **Partner platforms are most reliable** - Find Your Influence, Modash, Upfluence, etc.
4. **Compliance is essential** - GDPR, CAN-SPAM, and Pinterest ToS must be followed
5. **Email is primary channel** - Most professional and expected method
6. **Personalization drives results** - Generic mass outreach yields poor results
7. **Multi-platform research works** - Cross-reference Instagram, YouTube, websites
8. **Avoid scraping** - Explicitly prohibited and risks account termination
